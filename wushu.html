WUSHU

The Modern Marvel of Chinese Martial Arts

What is Wushu?

Wushu (Chinese: "martial art") is both an exhibition and a full-contact sport derived from traditional Chinese martial arts. While "Kung Fu" is a broader term, Wushu specifically refers to the standardized, highly athletic, and aesthetically spectacular routines (Taolu) and full-contact sparring (Sanda) that have been codified since 1949.

Wushu is renowned for its incredible acrobatics, speed, flexibility, and power. Practitioners perform jumps, kicks, and complex weapons forms (using swords, staffs, and spears) that push the limits of human physical capability, blending the ancient combat mechanics of Kung Fu with modern performance aesthetics.

Shaolin Wushu & The Founder

Much like traditional Kung Fu, the historical and spiritual roots of Wushu are inevitably tied to the Shaolin Temple in Henan province. Shaolin Wushu (or Shaolin Quan) is the cornerstone upon which much of modern Wushu was standardized.

Bodhidharma (Ta Mo)

Because Wushu is the modernized, sportified version of Chinese martial arts, its ultimate historical "founder" is still revered as the Indian Buddhist monk Bodhidharma (Ta Mo), the patriarch of the Shaolin Temple who introduced the physical exercises that evolved into Shaolin Kung Fu in the 5th/6th century.

The modern, standardized Wushu practiced in competitions today was developed in the mid-20th century by a committee of the greatest Chinese martial arts masters under the direction of the Chinese government. They synthesized the countless traditional styles—including prominent Shaolin forms—into a unified athletic syllabus.

Important Note on Shaolin Wushu

Shaolin Wushu retains the core philosophy of its ancient temple origins. The Shaolin monks believed that mastering difficult physical movements was a form of "moving meditation." In Wushu, the intense focus required to execute a perfect 720-degree spinning aerial kick is akin to deep Zen meditation.

It demands an unwavering union of intention (Yi) and force (Li). A master of Wushu doesn't just display physical prowess, but demonstrates total control over their internal energy and mind.
