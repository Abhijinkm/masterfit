KUNG FU

The Ancient Chinese Art of Mastery

What is Kung Fu?

Kung Fu, sometimes referred to as Wushu in modern contexts, is a broad term encompassing hundreds of styles of Chinese martial arts. It emphasizes continuous fluid movements, rapid explosive strikes, powerful footwork, and joint manipulation.

Beyond being merely a system of physical combat, Kung Fu is deeply rooted in ancient Chinese philosophy. It focuses heavily on personal discipline, mental focus, the cultivation of internal energy (Qi or Chi), and honorable moral character. At MasterFit, our Kung Fu training works to unite the mind, body, and spirit into an unbreakable whole.

Shaolin Kung Fu & The Founder

Among all Chinese martial arts, Shaolin Kung Fu is widely considered the oldest, most extensive, and most famous style. It traces its origins to the legendary Shaolin Temple situated in Henan province, China.

Bodhidharma (Ta Mo)

While the Shaolin Temple likely had some martial traditions beforehand, an Indian Buddhist monk named Bodhidharma (often referred to as Ta Mo in Chinese) is traditionally credited as the foundational patriarch of Shaolin Kung Fu. Arriving at the temple around the 5th or 6th century, he observed that the monks were physically weak and frequently fell asleep during rigorous meditation.

To remedy this, Bodhidharma introduced a physical conditioning regime and a series of dynamic exercises to strengthen their bodies and invigorate their minds. These initial exercises were designed to parallel the movements of animals in nature, and over centuries, they evolved into the highly sophisticated combat and spiritual techniques of Shaolin Kung Fu.

Important Note on Shaolin Kung Fu

Shaolin Kung Fu is fundamentally an intricate blend of Zen Buddhism (Chan) and martial arts (Quan), often summarized as "Chan Quan Yi Ti" (Zen and martial arts are one). The true master of Shaolin Kung Fu seeks spiritual enlightenment through immense physical exertion and strict discipline.

Its philosophical core dictates that all learned martial skills should only be used for strict self-defense, cultivating health, and the protection of the innocent—never for unprovoked aggression.
