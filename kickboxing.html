SHAOLIN KICKBOXING

The Ultimate Combat Sport (Sanda)

What is Shaolin Kickboxing?

Shaolin Kickboxing, commonly known in modern times as Sanda or Sanshou, is the official full-contact combat sport of Chinese martial arts. While traditional Kung Fu encompasses vast arrays of forms (taolu) and philosophical teachings, Sanda is the direct, practical application of these ancient combat techniques against a fully resisting opponent.

It is a highly dynamic form of kickboxing that not only incorporates powerful punches and devastating kicks but also seamlessly integrates fast-paced wrestling, takedowns, sweeps, and throws derived from ancient Chinese grappling arts (Shuai Jiao).

Shaolin Roots & The Founder

Just like its sister disciplines, the spiritual and historical lineage of Chinese kickboxing (Sanda) leads directly back to the Shaolin Temple.

Bodhidharma (Ta Mo)

While modern Sanda was formalized in the 20th century by the Chinese military to create a comprehensive hand-to-hand combat system, its foundational mechanics were laid by the Indian monk Bodhidharma at the Shaolin Temple. The physical conditioning and core combat principles he introduced—striking, kicking, and neutralizing an opponent's balance—evolved into the vast syllabus of Shaolin martial arts.

Historically, Shaolin warrior monks would test their skills in fierce, no-holds-barred matches called "Lei Tai" (fighting on a raised platform without ropes). These historical Lei Tai fights, where the goal was to strike or throw an opponent off the platform, are the direct predecessors to modern Shaolin Kickboxing competitions.

Important Note on Shaolin Kickboxing

While Shaolin Kickboxing (Sanda) is a brutal, full-contact combat sport, it retains the core of Shaolin philosophy: martial arts are a path to self-mastery, not a tool for bullying or aggression.

The addition of takedowns and throws to the standard kickboxing arsenal requires a practitioner to possess a deep understanding of leverage, balance, and the flow of energy—principles rooted deeply in ancient Shaolin Zen teachings. Master it to protect, not to destroy.
